Thayer Leads $45M Series B in TravelAI Platform

The investment accelerates TravelAI's expansion into corporate travel and international markets, positioning the company as the leading AI-native itinerary platform for a new generation of travelers.

Thayer Investment Partners today announced that it has led a $45 million Series B funding round in TravelAI, a next-generation platform leveraging artificial intelligence to transform how travelers plan, book, and experience journeys worldwide.

The round also included participation from existing investors Andreessen Horowitz and General Catalyst, along with new strategic investors from the airline and hospitality sectors. TravelAI will use the capital to accelerate product development, expand its corporate travel offering, and scale operations into eight new international markets over the next eighteen months.

A platform built for the AI-native traveler

TravelAI was founded in 2023 by former Google and Airbnb product leaders who believed that the core planning and booking experience — still largely dominated by legacy OTA infrastructure — was ripe for disruption. Their thesis: that a traveler willing to describe a trip in natural language should be able to receive a fully personalized, bookable itinerary in seconds, without navigating the fragmented interfaces that define most travel commerce today.

The platform now serves 2.4 million active users across 14 markets, with particularly strong adoption among frequent business travelers and premium leisure segments. Average booking value on TravelAI is 34% higher than comparable OTA transactions, reflecting the platform's ability to surface higher-quality options and reduce the friction that typically leads travelers to compromise on choice.

"We've been watching TravelAI since the seed round. What distinguishes them isn't just the quality of the AI — it's the conviction that the entire travel commerce stack needs to be rebuilt around the traveler's intent, not the supplier's inventory."

— Chris Hemmeter, Managing Partner, Thayer Investment Partners

Why now, and why Thayer

For Thayer, the investment represents a continuation of a thesis the firm has held since its founding: that the most durable travel technology companies are those that use technology to solve a problem travelers feel viscerally, rather than optimizing existing processes at the margins.

The timing is deliberate. Generative AI has crossed a capability threshold in the past eighteen months that makes truly conversational travel planning viable at scale for the first time. But the window for category leadership is narrow. Incumbents are moving quickly, and the companies that build distribution moats in the next two to three years will define the landscape for a decade.

The Corporate Opportunity

A significant portion of the Series B will fund TravelAI's expansion into managed corporate travel — a market that remains largely underserved by AI-native tools. Enterprise travel programs currently run on platforms designed in the early 2000s, and the gap between the experience a traveler can get on a consumer AI platform and what their corporate booking tool offers has never been wider.

TravelAI's enterprise product, currently in private beta with twelve Fortune 500 companies, integrates directly with existing TMC and expense infrastructure while surfacing an AI-native front end. Early data from the beta shows a 22% reduction in out-of-policy bookings and a meaningful increase in traveler satisfaction scores — two metrics that CFOs and travel managers care about in equal measure.

"Thayer brings something no other investor can: a direct line into the organizations that will actually buy what we're building. That network is worth as much as the capital."

— Elena Park, CEO, TravelAI

What comes next

With the Series B closed, TravelAI is expanding its engineering team from 45 to over 100 employees over the next year, with hiring concentrated in its San Francisco headquarters and a new London office that will anchor its European operations. International market launches are planned for Germany, Japan, Australia, and the UAE in the first half of 2026, with additional markets to follow as localization infrastructure scales.

Thayer's Chris Hemmeter will join TravelAI's board of directors as part of the investment, alongside existing board members from the company's Series A investors. The firm's portfolio team will work directly with TravelAI on enterprise channel development, leveraging Thayer's relationships across the hotel, airline, and corporate travel management sectors.

TravelAI is available at travelai.com and through enterprise procurement channels. For partnership inquiries, contact partnerships@travelai.com.
